Key Assumptions & Caveats

  • House NW 39th appears to be a personal property with costs flowing through the business (property taxes, utilities, maintenance).
  • Interest income ($161k in 2025) is on ~$2.5mm Merrill Lynch investment account — likely excess cash that would be excluded in a cash-free/debt-free transaction.
  • D&A dropped significantly in 2025 ($373k → $210k) — many assets may be fully depreciated. Verify against depreciation schedules.

Fleet Age & Replacement Risk

The core fleet of 5 operational vacuum trucks ranges from 2013–2023. Units #67 (2006) and #68 (1999) are aging backup assets. Unit #71 (2013, $45k R&M in 2025) is the most likely near-term replacement candidate. At $400–$500k per new vacuum truck, a single replacement represents a significant capital outlay relative to the company's EBITDA. The BOA loan ($523k remaining) finances units #72 and #73, declining ~$162k/year. A new buyer should budget $150–$250k/year in maintenance capex to sustain the fleet.

Key Questions for the March 20 Call

Organized by priority — ownership/transition questions first, then financial, operational, and growth.
